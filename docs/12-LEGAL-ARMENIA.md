# 12 — Legal & Business Setup (Armenia)

## Business Registration

### Option 1: Individual Entrepreneur (ԱՁ — Անհատ Ձեռնարկատեր)
- **Cost:** ~30,000 AMD (~$75) registration
- **Tax:** Turnover tax 1.5-5% (simplified) OR 18% income tax
- **Pros:** Cheapest to start, simplest bookkeeping
- **Cons:** Personal liability, less credible for investors
- **Best for:** Solo founder, pre-revenue, testing the idea

### Option 2: LLC (ՍՊԸ — Սdelays Պատdelays Delays Delays delays)
- **Cost:** ~100,000-150,000 AMD (~$250-400) registration
- **Minimum capital:** 1 AMD (yes, one dram)
- **Tax:** 18% corporate profit tax (but see IT exemption below)
- **Pros:** Limited liability, better for investors, more professional
- **Cons:** More paperwork, requires accountant
- **Best for:** Serious business, planning to raise investment

### Recommendation: Start as ԱՁ, convert to ՍՊԸ when generating revenue

## Armenia IT Tax Benefits

### IT Sector Exemptions (as of 2024-2025, verify current status)
- **Profit tax:** 0% for IT companies (was through 2025, may be extended)
- **Income tax for IT employees:** 10% flat rate (vs normal progressive up to 22%)
- **VAT exemption:** IT services exported outside Armenia are VAT-exempt (0% VAT)

### How to Qualify
1. Register as an IT company with the Ministry of High-Tech Industry
2. Primary activity must be IT/software development
3. Apply for IT certificate ( Տdelays Տ delays Delays ically through e-gov.am)

### Steps
- [ ] Consult with Armenian tax advisor/accountant (~$50-100 session)
- [ ] Register business entity (ԱՁ or ՍՊԸ)
- [ ] Apply for IT sector certificate
- [ ] Open business bank account (Ameriabank, Ardshinbank — both have good online banking)
- [ ] Register on tax.am for e-reporting

## Payment Processing in Armenia

### Stripe
- **Available in Armenia:** Yes (since 2023)
- **Payout:** To Armenian bank account in USD or AMD
- **Fees:** 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction
- You need: registered business + Armenian bank account

### App Store / Play Store
- Payments go to your linked bank account
- Apple pays monthly, 45 days after month end
- Google pays monthly, 15th of following month
- Both support Armenian bank accounts

## Legal Documents Required

### For the App
- [ ] **Terms of Service** — governs user behavior, content ownership, limitations
- [ ] **Privacy Policy** — required by App Store, Play Store, GDPR
- [ ] **Cookie Policy** — required for EU users (web)
- [ ] **Acceptable Use Policy** — what users can/cannot generate
- [ ] **DMCA Policy** — copyright takedown process (required for US users)

### Key Legal Points for AI Content
- Users own rights to their AI-generated content (but check model-specific licenses)
- You are not liable for content users generate
- You reserve the right to remove content that violates terms
- NSFW content policy (required for App Store approval)
- Data retention policy (how long you keep generations)

### Templates
You can start with templates from:
- Termly.io (free privacy policy generator)
- iubenda.com (GDPR-compliant generator)
- Have a lawyer review before launch (~$200-500 in Armenia)

## International Tax Considerations

### EU Customers (VAT)
- If you sell B2C to EU customers, you may need to charge VAT
- Threshold: varies by country, but Stripe Tax can handle this automatically
- Stripe Tax: auto-calculates and collects VAT (+0.5% per transaction)
- Alternative: use a Merchant of Record (Paddle, Lemon Squeezy) — they handle all tax

### US Customers
- No sales tax on digital services in most states (check specific states)
- No withholding tax issues if you have proper tax treaty documentation

### Recommendation
Use **Stripe Tax** or switch to **Lemon Squeezy** (Merchant of Record) to avoid dealing with international tax compliance yourself. Lemon Squeezy handles all tax, VAT, and compliance for you — they are the seller, not you. Fee is ~5% + $0.50 per transaction (higher than Stripe but zero tax headache).
